  • Time
    In the beginning, there was no time.
  • Or, rather, there was always time.
  • We, humanity, were constrained by the temporality of the present.
  • Actions could only be done sequentially and they took, essentially, as long as they took.
  • Environmental factors dictated our life, from sundown to sunup.
  • Modern means of mechanization had not yet begun to annihilate time. (Solnit 7)
  • We existed as fish swimming through time, with no more need to define and demarcate it than a fish has to define water.
  • Time was both everywhere and nowhere.
  • Revolution
    Though the assault of mechanism on time had begin decades earlier, with Greek philosophers attacking its veritable basis (On Truth), the industrial revolution struck a fearsome blow against old Kronos.
